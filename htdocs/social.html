Connected Through Screens

Social well-being is about maintaining good relationships with others. This is very difficult nowadays, since we can only interact with others through a screen. We used to be able to easily talk and interact with friends face-to-face, so how will we go about this now in this situation?

Why Should We Care?

While it may not seem to be the topmost priority and something that people actively look after compared to physical and mental health, it is still very important nonetheless. Being social creatures, it is important to interact with others to avoid being socially isolated and lonely. We have at least some desire to interact and connect with others. If we aren't able or allowed to for long periods of time, there may be unwanted and negative feelings of isolation and further withdrawal.

What Can We Do?

In all aspects of life we're bound to meet and make connections with people, but nourishing those connections are what matter most, so here are a few things to keep in mind to maintain good social well-being (specifically during this pandemic):

  1. Try to make new connections: keep making friends wherever you go. Who knows, maybe they could be your new best friends! Try to interact with your class/schoolmates more, whether for random conversations or helping each other out. Even if it's online, it doesn't make it any less meaningful!

  2. Schedule times to hangout or do things together: if you're the type to enjoy group study sessions, then this may be right up your alley. Schedule some time to review or study with your friends. It could help enrich your learnings together. If you prefer to study alone, do not fret! You could instead schedule hangouts to just check-in and talk or play fun games!

  3. Keep healthy relationships: not all relationships we have are beneficial to us. Healthy relationships rely on trust, respect and communication. Having an honest conversation with the person you're facing an issue with is crucial in healthy relationships. Now, how they react to this conversation (violent reaction or understanding, etc.) would help you assess if it is healthy.

  4. Remember to take care of yourself: as important as it is to interact with others, we all need time for ourselves to rest. Remember to rest when needed to be able to recharge your social battery. Practicing self-care is also important.
