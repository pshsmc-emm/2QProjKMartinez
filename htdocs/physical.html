Are You FITT?

Physical well-being, according to the Australian National University, is maintaining a healthy lifestyle and taking care of our body to avoid negative impacts on our health. Exercise is very important, especially during this pandemic. Let’s take a look at a simple fitness principle that we can apply to keep ourselves fit and achieve what goals we want to have in exercising.

F (Frequency)

As the name suggests, it’s how often you plan to exercise. This will be decided by the type of exercise, how fit you currently are, goals and the next part, intensity. This will help avoid overworking the body to not lose any of the progress already made. Here are some of the recommended frequencies for teenagers in different exercises:

  • Cardio (raises heart rate): 2-3 times a week
  • Muscle Strength (improves strength of muscles): 3 times a week
  • Bone Strength (helps strengthen bones): 3 times a week

I (Intensity)

This is how vigorous you exercise and how much you have to exert for it. This also helps avoid straining or overworking the body, which may lead to injuries or soreness. This can be classified into Beginners (Low Intensity), Intermediate (Mid Intensity) and Advanced (High Intensity).

How Do We Measure Intensity?

To know that, we first need to know our Maximum Heart Rate or MHR which is measured by subtracting our current age from the number 220.

  • Low Intensity is when our Maximum Heart Rate (MHR) reaches 45-54%.
  • Intermediate or Mid Intensity is when the MHR reachers 55-69%.
  • Advanced or High Intensity is when the MHR is 70% or higher.

T (Time)

This is the duration of time you allot for yourself to exercise. Usually, high intensity exercises have shorter time than low intensity exercises. Experts recommend teenagers to have at least 60 minutes of exercise everyday.

T (Type)

Type is the exercise that will be performed based on your goals. It could be Cardio, Strength, Flexibility, and more. Choosing the type you will do depends on what you want to achieve through exercising (endurance, gain muscle, lose/gain weight). For example, 30 minutes a day of low-to-moderate intensity activities such as dancing, climbing stairs or pleasure walking is good in order to establish a healthy habit.

Wait There's More!

While the FITT Principle is a guide to regular exercise, the simple things we do everyday such as chores (sweeping the floor) are actually counted to help keep us fit! As mentioned above, having good physical well-being is a lifestyle which includes things more than exercise. Here’s an additional list of simple things one can do to stay physically fit:

  • Have a healthy and balanced diet
  • Get enough sleep and follow a proper sleep schedule
  • Drink enough water to stay hydrated
  • Get your daily dose of Vitamin D from sunlight
